## About this content pattern

A community of practice sign-up page

### Content type (Drupal):

This is built using a Basic page

### Pattern status

Not user tested

## Pattern sections

### Summary

At the top of each Community page, there will be a summary.

This should tell the user what the purpose of the community is.

This could come from a vision or mission statement.

### Who this community is for

The purpose of this section is to help people understand if this is the community for them.

This should be a bullet list of roles or jobs that people in the community might be doing.

Optionally, you could add a bullet list of what typically happens in a community meeting, such as:

> In a typical meeting, we might:
- share our recent design work
- have a guest speaker to talk about a specialist topic or project
- discuss common issues we are facing
> 

Another optional addition is a link to any guide available on the CDPS website about the discipline or practice. Follow the [Welsh Government Linking from within sentences (inline) guide](https://www.gov.wales/linking-within-sentences-inline) to do this. For example:

> The [UX, UI and interaction design guide](https://digitalpublicservices.gov.wales/guidance-and-standards/meet-user-needs/ux-ui-and-interaction-design) tells you about the types of design and the role that they play when it comes to developing services or products. These are the kinds of things we talk about.
> 

### How we meet

The purpose of this section is to tell people what to expect once they join the community. Let them know about:

- any in-person or virtual meet-ups
- any online chat, forum or messaging platform they’ll get access to

### Culture

The purpose of this section is to make it clear how members should conduct themselves.

### Community lead

This section makes the community sign-up page more human. It includes the community lead’s:

- name
- email address

### Join the community

The purpose of the section is to introduce and present the sign-up form.

The heading should say:

> ‘Join the community’
> 

or 

> Join the [community name]
> 

It should not just be ‘Join’

The introductory text is optional, but it shouldn’t be more than one sentence

The form is built in the CRM (Hubspot) and is embedded on the page within the content management system (Drupal).

The fields on the form are:

- First name
- Last name
- Email address
- Your organisation
- Job title
- Language preference
- Location
- What would you like to share, learn or ask?
- Where did you hear about this community?

The button text will say ‘Join’

### Thank you page

Once the user submits a form, they should get a thank you message. 

For accessibility reasons, this should be on a separate page that loads with a:

- new page title, such as ‘Thank you for joining [community name]’
- information about what happens next
