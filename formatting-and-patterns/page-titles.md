> Think about user intent when naming pages on the website so that our content answers their question, or tells them what they need to know.

This doesn’t mean writing your content as questions, but it helps you to know:

- which keywords to include in your content
- which questions your content needs to answer (and which ones are most important)

The most important points, for example, could be in your page title and page summary. 

The rest could be used in H2 headings throughout your page.

## Tools

If you don’t have user research to work from, these tools can help you do this.

[**Google Trends**](https://trends.google.com/trends/) for finding breakout keywords.

[**Answer The Public**](https://answerthepublic.com/) for finding keywords phrased as questions.

Use Google search console to look at:

- what people search foe and don’t find
- what existing content is receiving search traffic on our website
