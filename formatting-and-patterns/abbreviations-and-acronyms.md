# Abbreviations and acronyms

The first time you use an abbreviation or an acronym (an abbreviation you pronounce as a word, like Acas) write it out in full on each page unless it’s well known, like UK, NHS or BBC. This includes government departments or schemes and arm’s length bodies such as us. Then refer to it by initials.

For example, write:

> The Centre for Digital Public Services (CDPS) is an arm's length body of the Welsh government responsible for delivering the Digital Strategy for Wales. CDPS was founded in 2020 with a vision to create public services in Wales that are joined up, safe and easy to use.
> 

Do not use full stops in abbreviations: NHS, not N.H.S.

Do not use an abbreviation if you’re not going to use it again later in the text.
