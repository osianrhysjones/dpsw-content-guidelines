# Headings

Related content types: Blog posts (../../Content%20types/Blog%20posts%20fdef74657c1446d6a07d10876cca1e63.md), Resources (../../Content%20types/Resources%20e71e7cc64bf74a23b7ebec34da17e324.md), Course (../../Content%20types/Course%20141da5b9f33646a6bf6b654eb076567c.md), Resource page (../../Content%20types/Resource%20page%20ce90c3cf781f4b6db9bbb3378f72be39.md), Board minutes (../../Content%20types/Board%20minutes%20a273ded5a9d94b039e26c08dd13e074c.md), Community of practice (standard page) (../../Content%20types/Community%20of%20practice%20(standard%20page)%2042643f84d46e4e73af0519bb40af96d9.md)

Use headings and subheadings to break up your content. This makes your content:

- easier for people to read
- more accessible for those using assistive technology like screen readers

Headings should be short, clear and descriptive because this helps people to understand what content is there and find the content they need.

Used subheadings roughly every 2 paragraphs to help users to scan your content. Subheadings should always be in sentence case.

Front-load your headings where possible. This means putting the words people are looking for at the start. Statements work better than questions and they let you put the keyword first.

> ✅ **Good example:**
> Project activity

> ❌ **Bad example:**
> Want to know what is happening? Look at the project activity

Do not be afraid to be creative to make sure you convey what’s in the text below although **avoid presenting headings as questions**. 'Who can apply' not 'Who can apply?'

## The imperative and headings

A heading, in most cases, should use the gerund:

‘Applying for a refund’ 

> Gerunds are a flexible, useful type of word because they enable us to speak and write about actions in a more abstract way, positioning those actions as reasons for specific outcomes, hobbies, and receivers of subjects’ actions.
> 

Otherwise, if it’s more descriptive, use ‘How to apply for a refund’

This is because the imperative (which in English is the same as the infinitive) should be reserved for calls to action, such as links or buttons.
