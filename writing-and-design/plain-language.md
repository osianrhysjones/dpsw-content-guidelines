# Plain language at CDPS

Plain language is recognised internationally as the best practice - here is some messaging and resources about plain language.

---

> ⭐
> Information that isn’t clear isn’t accessible.

A big part of accessibility is having clear information, in accessible formats and on journeys that make sense to users. In Wales that means adopting both:

- [plain English](https://plainlanguagenetwork.org/plain-language/what-is-plain-language/)
- [Cymraeg Clir](https://www.bangor.ac.uk/canolfan-bedwyr/cymraegclir)

Complex language or user journeys will immediately make a product or service inaccessible - and fail to meet standards.

All designers will be aware of accessibility issues but content designers have a particular focus on content and the specific technical aspects of best practice.
