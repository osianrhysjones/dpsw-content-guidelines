# How we design content at CDPS

How we make sure the information we create is readable, clear, findable and actionable.

---

---

## We design for user needs

We always start with a user need. Ask: 

- why am I writing this and who is it for?
- what do they need to know and when?
- what don't they need to know?
- what format is best for them?

We’re not being awkward. We want to meet user needs and business goals.

## We collaborate

We're an integral part of design and development teams attached to a product or service, from beginning to end.

We help the organisation to work together and collaborate on content. 

We work with delivery managers, user researchers, other designers, subject experts and stakeholders. 

We also work with translators to design bilingually.

We build consensus and empathy.  

The sooner we’re involved, the more chance there is we all know the purpose of the content and what user needs it meets.

## We design content for accessibility

This includes thinking about how content can be accessible and inclusive for people who:

- use screen readers
- are blind or have sight loss
- have conditions which make it harder for them to process information (such as Dyscalculia (Dis - Cal - Coo - Lia) )
- English is an additional language
- are under stress or in crisis
- are in marginalised groups

## We prevent harm

We’re aware of the power of what we say and how we say it. 

By understanding our diverse users, we can take steps to prevent harm in our content.

## We advocate a content-first approach

A Word document or a Google doc is a prototype.

This means creating prototypes that start with content rather than visuals.

This way, structure and messaging is built on user needs. And avoids content designers having to fill boxes that are already in place.

See more about [how to prototype content](prototyping-contentmd.md).

## We support people and businesses to succeed

When we design content and services internally, we also help our colleagues to understand:

- what we’re trying to accomplish
- what we need people need to learn, do or understand
- what problem the content is solving for us
