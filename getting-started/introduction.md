# Creating content: an introduction

Designing content is about using data and evidence to give users timely, clear information in the best format to meet their needs.

---

---

## Get started with a content design mindset

- Be aware that you're creating content all the time
- Define clearly what you're trying to accomplish
- Understand the users of your content and their needs
- Align what you're trying to achieve with your users and their needs
- Collaborate with others. Test assumptions. Ask for feedback. Iterate. Iterate. Iterate.

## Designing content, not copywriting

Designing content is not just creativity and style. It is not copywriting.

It’s about:

- understanding our users, their contexts and designing for their needs
- understanding business goals
- creating the environment for good content
- how information is structured and ordered
- the architecture of the information
- the user journeys
- being inclusive and accessible

## Why you should design content

The content (information) is the thing people use your product or service for. 

Thinking like a designer when you develop information:

- improves the experience of your users
- reduces noise
- increases engagement.

It makes your content more:

- effective and usable
- clearer, more findable and useful
- accessible and inclusive.

In the long run, it saves you time and effort because you stop

- producing content that meets no need
- doing the same thing again and again

### Accessibility and inclusivity

Information that isn’t clear isn’t accessible. 

A big part of accessibility is having clear information, in accessible formats and on journeys that make sense to users.

Complex language or user journeys will immediately make a product or service inaccessible - and fail to meet standards.

All designers will be aware of accessibility issues but content designers have a particular focus on content, obviously, and specific technical aspects of best practice.

### Quality and consistency

**Style guides and editorial standards** to keep content clear and consistent. These should be used by the whole organisation.

## When to design content

A content design approach is relevant to all types of communication. This includes channels such as:

- web content
- emails, notifications
- alerts
- mail (letters)

It might also include formats such as:

- video
- audio
- graphics

It’s anything else which conveys information or meaning to service user. What’s important is that users get the right information, at the right time, in the right format.

> Consider content from the start of your project. Don't put Lorem Ipsum, or AI-generated content into a visual design and assume content can be added later.
