# Translation and bilingual design strategy

This translation strategy sets out our approach to translation in CDPS and supports wider content strategy work.

---

---

## Principles

Translation is an integral part of planning, writing and managing bilingual content.

This means it should be integrated into existing workflows wherever possible.

Translation can become a way to accelerate moves towards better content workflows and processes in CDPS.

## Translation paths

Our strategy is based on seeing our existing workload as 3 enabling pathways:

### **1. Llwybr llydan (a broad path)**

Use this for:

- Working collaboratively with research, design and Comms, embedding translation into existing processes for planning, writing and managing bilingual content
- Partner delivery projects

Examples:

- Supporting desk research, such as auditing and reviewing Welsh content
- Peer review (2i)
- Trio-editing
- Trio writing
- Content types such as: Service manual article, Slide decks, Email translation, Blog posts, Project pages, Jobs, Course, Event, Standard

### **2. Llwybr tarw (a straight, no-nonsense path)**

Use this for:

- Enabling Welsh speakers of all levels to use software to write and quality assure Welsh content but also get a 2i in Welsh.
- Give Welsh speakers tools to support them create content bilingually or translate their own content
- This must be used carefully within content creation and publishing processes.
- Providing a peer review (2i) in place for Welsh content

Example content:

- Social media posts
- Presentation decks
- Short emails

### **3. Llwybr cul (a narrow path)**

Use this for:

- more traditional translation workflows, such as documents
- working with a high volume of words

Example content:

- official, corporate documents that will be shared in a document format
- Information such as Research reports, End-of-project reports, annual reports, Board minutes
- Quick turnaround corporate comms, such as weeknotes

## Our strategy

- Become more efficient:
    - Use tools and software - such as Phrase Translation Management - to make traditional written translation tasks entering as efficient as possible.
    - Acknowledge that some translation tasks will need to happen in ‘Llwybr cul’ but that this is not where we get the most value from our in-house translators.
- Become more collaborative:
    - Identify translation tasks that enter the Llwybr Cul that could be on Llwybr Tarw or Llwybr Llydan.
    - Involve translation in the planning, writing and reviewing of bilingual content
    - Create and document QA process for all translation work
    - Apply content design methods and techniques in a bilingual context wherever possible
    - Work with colleagues across CDPS to document, implement and continuously improve our content strategy (governance, workflows)
- Give Welsh-speaking staff confidence and tools to plan and write content bilingually and peer review (2i) each others’ work. This means:
    - offering 2i as part of the translation service
    - offering suggested tools, such as Cysgliad or Machine translation software
    - Sharing suggested bilingual glossaries and style guides
    - offering content review or knowledge-sharing opportunities across CDPS
- Upskilling our knowledge
    - Upskill translators in content design practices
    - Make sure staff in CDPS are aware of translation capacity and preferred ways of working
